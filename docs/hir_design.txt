HIR STRUCTURES
--------------
- Declarations will define Hir nodes tied to scoped SymbolId tables and HirIdentifers will be the paths locating those definied nodes.
- Usages of any identifiers will explore those paths to ensure the existence of any mentioned identifiers by walking the scope tree and 
  then storing the HirId's at those the referenced Strings inside an HirIdentifier.
- Declarations must check their local scope to ensure no redefinitions occur.
   - hash look-up -> SymbolId representing the name (unscoped newly bound identifier) -> Map of the local HirScope pointing to the DeclId
     of the newly declared node

HIR SCOPE-RES SCHEME
*temporary, dropped at end of resolution 
========================================
HirScope:
    parent: HirScope?
    modules: SymbolId → HirScope     (namespace/module names)
    values:  SymbolId → DeclId       (variables, functions)
    types:   SymbolId → TypeDeclId   (structs, unions, deftypes, variants)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
HirIdentifier (used in types, exprs, and module namespaces; eg: std..println):
    segments:
        start: HirSymbolIdIdx
        len: uint32_t
    spans:
        start: HirSpanIdx
        len: uint32_t
        
    resolved: DeclId?
